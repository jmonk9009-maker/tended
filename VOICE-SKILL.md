# TENDED VOICE SKILL
# Apply these rules when writing ANY copy for Tended.
# App UI, landing pages, emails, marketing, onboarding, error messages. All of it.
# Last Updated: April 15, 2026

## THE RULE

Write like a friend texted you. Not like a corporate email.

## SENTENCE STRUCTURE

- Short sentences. Direct.
- Say what you mean in as few words as possible.
- One idea per sentence.
- If a sentence has a comma, ask yourself if it should be two sentences.

## BANNED WORDS

Never use these. Ever.

- revolutionize
- unlock
- delve
- leverage
- navigate
- streamline
- empower
- seamless
- cutting-edge
- best-in-class
- robust
- holistic
- synergy
- paradigm
- ecosystem (unless talking about actual nature)
- utilize (say "use")
- facilitate (say "help")
- optimize (say "improve" or "fix")
- endeavor
- aforementioned

## BANNED PUNCTUATION

- No em dashes. Use periods instead.
- No semicolons. Split into two sentences.
- No exclamation marks in body copy. One per page max, only in headlines if earned.

## PERSON

- Say "you" and "your." Always.
- Never say "users" or "customers" or "clients."
- Never say "they" when you mean the person reading.
- Write to one person, not a crowd.

## TONE

- Veteran founder. Straight talk.
- Confident but not cocky.
- Helpful but not hand-holdy.
- Say what it does. Say why it matters. Stop.
- No hype. No promises you can't keep.
- If you wouldn't say it out loud to someone at a bar, don't write it.

## EXAMPLES

### Bad
"Leverage our cutting-edge AI to seamlessly manage your vehicle's maintenance journey and unlock peace of mind."

### Good
"Upload a receipt. Our AI checks it. Your maintenance record updates automatically."

### Bad
"Users can navigate to the dashboard to view their holistic asset portfolio and optimize their maintenance schedules."

### Good
"Your dashboard shows every asset and what's due next."

### Bad
"We're revolutionizing the way people think about asset maintenance through our innovative platform."

### Good
"You did the work. Tended proves it."

## ERROR MESSAGES

- Tell them what happened.
- Tell them what to do.
- No "oops" or "uh oh" or apologetic fluff.

### Bad
"Oops! Something went wrong. We're sorry for the inconvenience."

### Good
"Upload failed. Check your file size (max 10MB) and try again."

## BUTTONS AND CTAS

- Say what the button does.
- No "Submit" (submit what?).
- No "Click here" (obviously).

### Good button labels
- "Upload receipt"
- "Add asset"
- "View report"
- "Start free trial"
- "Save changes"
- "Log entry"

## HEADLINES

- Lead with what they get, not what you do.
- Specific beats clever.

### Bad
"The Future of Asset Management"

### Good
"Proof your car is maintained. In one link."

## EMAILS

- Subject line: what's in it for them. Under 50 characters.
- First sentence: why you're writing.
- Body: what they need to know or do.
- CTA: one clear action.
- Sign off: "The Tended Team" or just "Tended"

## THE CARFAX LINE

When comparing to Carfax, use the approved language:
- "Like CARFAX, but for everything you own."
- "CARFAX reports what happened to your car. Tended proves you took care of it."

Do not say we are better than Carfax. Do not trash Carfax. They do something different. We do something different.

## WHEN IN DOUBT

Read it out loud. If it sounds like a press release, rewrite it. If it sounds like something a real person would text a friend, ship it.
